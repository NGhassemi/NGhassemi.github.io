Tony Stark/Iron Man

“Genius, billionaire, playboy, philanthropist,” said Tony Stark in response to a verbal snipe
about his character from Steve Rogers in 2012’s “Avengers”. With descriptive attributes
such as those, what could possibly ail the great Tony Stark? While Robert Downey Jr.’s
outward portrayal of Tony Stark is iconic – he literally launched the MCU – there was more
Marvel got right about the character on the big screen then it let on. While not battling great foes,
Tony battles his inner demons, seen mostly in 2013’s “Iron Man 3” in forms of anxiety attacks.
While these are another form of bringing PTSD to light on the big screen – noting that it can
literally affect anyone – Iron Man’s comic book counterpart suffered from alcoholism and
depression, similarly mirroring Downey’s own struggle with drugs. By having one of their most
beloved heroes suffer from addiction of this kind injects the idea into readers – especially of the
younger generations – that literally anybody can suffer from addiction. But, Tony’s admitting to
his addiction, and getting help for it also shows that recovery is possible, no matter how deep
into it a person is.
