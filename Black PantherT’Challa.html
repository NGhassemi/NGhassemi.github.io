Black Panther/T'Challa

The first African superhero, Black Panther was released in 1966,
amid extremely tense racial relations in the United States.
In an interview about creating the character, Stan Lee
said “A good many of our people here in America are not white.
You’ve got to recognize that you’ve got to include them in
whatever you do” (2018). Black Panther struck a chord throughout
the African American community, from adults to children. There was
finally a superhero who took the place of Thor or Spiderman in the
toy boxes of African American children. The massive success of the Black
Panther series on and off screen stresses the importance
of representation to the silenced other, creating a hero that the
African American community could finally, truly, identify with.
